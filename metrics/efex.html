Evaluación de la Efectividad de las Reglas en Falco Runtime Security: Un Enfoque Basado en Comportamientos

Calcular la efectividad de las reglas de una herramienta de seguridad como Falco implica evaluar qué tan bien las reglas detectan incidentes relevantes y evitan falsos positivos. Aquí tienes un enfoque estructurado para calcular esta efectividad:

Falco rules enable real-time monitoring and detection of anomalous or suspicious behaviors based on a predefined set of conditions. Evaluating the effectiveness of these rules not only ensures their proper configuration but also helps optimize security coverage by identifying gaps in detection. Evaluation Scenario In this case, we are working with a total of 80 rules, divided as follows: 40 vendor rules (preconfigured by the provider). 40 custom rules (tailored to the specific environment). A thorough analysis has been conducted based on the ability of the rules to detect specific behaviors by replicating conditions associated with each one. The results are as follows: 40 custom rules are fully effective as they successfully detect the configured behaviors. Out of the 40 vendor rules, only 30 are effective, while the remaining 10 fail to detect replicated behaviors. This allows us to infer that, while preconfigured rules provide a good starting point, customized rules tend to be more effective since they are specifically designed to address the needs of the environment. Evaluation Methodology The effectiveness of each rule was assessed using a behavior-based approach: Understanding the rule: Comprehend the activity the rule aims to detect (e.g., unauthorized socket creation, container escape attempts, or modifications to critical files). Behavior replication: Simulate the conditions the rule is designed to detect, ensuring these correspond to real or potential attack scenarios. Validation: Observe whether Falco generates alerts when replicating the behavior. This helps determine whether the rule is effective or requires adjustments.
